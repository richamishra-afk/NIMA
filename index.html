The three wings

One centre, three kinds of memory

NIMA is not a single room or a one-time digitisation drive. It is a permanent centre built from three strands, each with its own name, mandate, and audience, and none of them complete without the others.

Keeps
स्

SMRITI

Scholarly Memory and Records for Institutional Traditions and Inheritance

The archival wing. It holds the documents, photographs, recordings, and objects that prove how Nirma was built, kept to international standards for researchers and for the generations still to come.

Shows
दृ

DRISHYA

Digital Repository for Interpretation, Storytelling, Heritage and Young Audiences

The public and digital wing. It turns what the archive holds into exhibitions, a digital portal, and experiences that reach people who will never walk through a reading room door.

Listens
वा

VARTA

Voices, Archives, Reflections, Testimonies, Achievement

The living voice of the centre. It records the people who built Nirma while they are still here to tell it, and keeps the memory renewed through conversation.

This walkthrough follows two of them at work. First SMRITI, the craft of keeping. Then VARTA, the craft of listening.

SMRITI at work

Watch one record enter the archive

Every item follows the same seven steps. Here a single page is found in its box, pulled out, stamped, catalogued, scanned into a digital copy, sealed for preservation, and finally opened for anyone with reason to consult it.

VARTA at work

Five movements from voice to legacy

SMRITI keeps what was written down. VARTA reaches for what was never written, the memory that lives only in the people who carry it. Its own name maps the way it works.

V
Voices

Finding the people who were there

Employees, faculty, administrators, alumni, dealers, and collaborators are invited to speak in their own words. The search is for the voices that carry what no file ever recorded.

A
Archives

Every conversation kept

Each recording is treated as a permanent record from the moment it is made, captured cleanly and prepared to enter the archive rather than fade after a single listen.

R
Reflections

Room to look back

People are given space to make meaning of what they lived, the changes they saw, the challenges they met, and the values they carried through the years.

T
Testimonies

The Milestone Conversations

Formal oral histories honour those who have given thirty, forty, or fifty years of service, so that decades of knowledge are not lost at the moment of retirement.

A
Achievement

A life carried forward

The contribution of each person is recognised and set into the institutional record, so that individual effort becomes part of the collective story.

A voice recorded through VARTA does not stay a recording. It is preserved in SMRITI and opened to the world through DRISHYA, and the conversation becomes part of the permanent record.

VARTA records→SMRITI preserves→DRISHYA shares

How the three hold together

One thread of memory

The wings are not separate departments doing separate work. What VARTA hears and SMRITI keeps is what DRISHYA shows. Together they form a single memory that is preserved, interpreted, and continuously renewed.

Memory is not only kept. It is passed forward.
